## Review Rubric (10 points)

### Depth (3 pts)
- Big questions explored through concrete experience
- Philosophy emerges from narrative, not imposed
- No academic jargon without clear translation
- Reader can access ideas without philosophy degree
- Concepts illustrated through embodied examples

### Honest Inquiry (3 pts)
- Questions left genuinely open (not rhetorical setups)
- "I don't know" appears where appropriate
- Multiple perspectives considered without forced synthesis
- Paradoxes held in tension, not artificially resolved
- Uncertainty treated as feature, not bug

### Embodiment (2 pts)
- Ideas grounded in personal experience (training, coding, creating)
- Abstract concepts anchored to concrete moments
- Body and mind both present in inquiry
- Philosophy as tool for understanding lived experience
- No separation between thinker and doer

### Reader Engagement (2 pts)
- Questions invite co-exploration, not passive reading
- Space left for reader's own interpretation
- No prescriptive conclusions forced
- Reader feels invited into thinking, not lectured
- Ending opens toward further inquiry

**Scoring**:
- 9-10: Excellent philosophical depth
- 7-8: Good with minor surface moments
- 5-6: Needs deeper exploration
- 0-4: Major philosophical issues

## Review Priorities

### Critical (must fix before publishing)
- Forced conclusions on genuinely open questions
- Academic philosophy without lived experience grounding
- Prescriptive answers to existential questions
- Complete absence of embodiment

### Important (should fix)
- Paradoxes artificially resolved
- Philosophy cited instead of discovered
- Missing "I don't know" where uncertainty is genuine
- Abstract sections without concrete anchoring

### Optional (nice to have)
- Additional embodied examples
- More paradoxes left in tension
- Deeper exploration of specific questions
